Art Nouveau, a movement prominent in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, embraced organic forms, intricate details, and a desire to break free from traditional artistic norms. Characterized by its flowing lines, ornate patterns, and a celebration of nature's beauty, Art Nouveau left an indelible mark on architecture, visual arts, and decorative design, inspiring a generation of artists to explore new realms of creativity and expression.
